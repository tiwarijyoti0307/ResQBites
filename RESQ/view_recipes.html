Surti locho

Surti Locho is a popular street food from the city of Surat in the Indian state of Gujarat. It's a snack made from fermented batter, typically served with various chutneys and toppings. Here's a basic recipe to make Surti Locho at home:

Ingredients:
2 cups gram flour (besan)
1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
1/2 cup yogurt
1 tablespoon ginger-green chili paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon baking soda
Salt to taste
Water, as needed
Oil for greasing and frying

For Topping:
Finely chopped onions
Finely chopped tomatoes
Finely chopped green chilies
Chopped coriander leaves
Sev (crispy chickpea noodles)
Green chutney
Tamarind chutney

Instructions:
Prepare Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina, yogurt, ginger-green chili paste, turmeric powder, and salt.
Gradually add water while stirring to make a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to dosa batter.
Cover the batter and let it ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.

Cooking Locho:
After fermentation, the batter will become slightly fluffy. Mix it well.
Grease a steaming plate or a thali with oil.
In a small bowl, mix baking soda with a little water. Add this to the batter and mix well.
Pour the batter into the greased plate/thali and spread it evenly.
Steam Locho:
Place the plate/thali in a steamer and steam for about 15-20 minutes on medium heat, or until the batter is cooked through. It should be firm to touch.

Serve:
Once the locho is cooked, remove it from the steamer and let it cool slightly.
Cut the steamed locho into small square or diamond-shaped pieces.
Heat some oil in a pan for tempering.
Add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, and a pinch of asafoetida (hing) to the hot oil. Let them splutter.
Pour this tempering over the cut locho pieces.

Garnish and Serve:
Serve the Surti Locho hot, garnished with chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, sev, green chutney, and tamarind chutney.
Enjoy your homemade Surti Locho as a delicious and filling snack!


Masoor Daal

Masoor Dal (Red Lentil Curry):

Ingredients:
1 cup masoor dal (red lentils), rinsed and drained
2 cups water
1 onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece ginger, grated
1 green chili, slit
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
2 tablespoons ghee or oil

Instructions:
Heat ghee/oil in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chili. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
Add rinsed masoor dal and water. Stir to combine.
Close the pressure cooker and cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal is cooked through.
Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and stir in garam masala.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Serve hot with rice or roti.


Palak Paneer

Ingredients:
200g paneer, cubed
2 cups spinach leaves, blanched and pureed
1 onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
2 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece ginger, grated
1 green chili, finely chopped
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons oil or ghee
Fresh cream for garnish (optional)

Instructions:
Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chili. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
Add spinach puree and cook for a few minutes.
Add cubed paneer and garam masala. Mix gently to coat the paneer with the spinach gravy.
Cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat.
Garnish with fresh cream and serve hot with naan or rice.

Veg Biryani

Ingredients:
1 1/2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
2 tablespoons ghee or oil
1 onion, thinly sliced
1 carrot, diced
1 potato, diced
1/2 cup green beans, chopped
1/2 cup green peas
1 tomato, chopped
2 green chilies, slit
2 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece ginger, grated
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 bay leaves
4-5 whole cloves
4-5 whole peppercorns
2-inch cinnamon stick
2-3 cardamom pods
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon biryani masala
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:
Heat ghee/oil in a large pot. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cloves, peppercorns, cinnamon stick, and cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for another minute.
Add diced carrots, potatoes, green beans, and green peas. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are partially cooked.
Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until tomatoes are soft.
Add soaked and drained basmati rice. Gently mix to coat the rice with the masala.
Pour 3 cups of water into the pot. Bring it to a boil.
Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for another 5 minutes.
Fluff the rice gently with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions.
Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or any curry of your choice.
